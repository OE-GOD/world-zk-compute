Protocol

MOOUSER converts ML inference into an arithmetic circuit, then generates a zero-knowledge proof that the circuit was evaluated correctly. The verifier checks the proof without re-executing the model.

1

Circuit Compilation

The ML model (tree ensemble, linear model, or neural network) is compiled into a layered arithmetic circuit. Each decision node, matrix multiply, or comparison becomes a gate. An 88-tree XGBoost model produces an 88-layer DAG circuit.

2

Proof Generation

The GKR interactive proof protocol is applied layer-by-layer, reducing the claim about the output to claims about the input. Hyrax polynomial commitments bind the prover to specific intermediate values. The proof is non-interactive via Fiat-Shamir (Poseidon sponge).

3

Verification

The verifier replays the transcript, checks sumcheck rounds, and verifies polynomial evaluation proofs. No access to model weights or input data required. Verification is O(n log n) in circuit depth — independent of the number of gates.

4

Receipt

A signed ECDSA receipt binds the proof to a timestamp and verifier identity. Receipts are recorded in a Merkle-tree transparency log — like Certificate Transparency, but for AI decisions. Tamper-evident and publicly auditable.

Guarantees

The proof binds four things together cryptographically. Tampering with any one of them invalidates the proof.

PropertyGuaranteeMechanism
Model identityCircuit hash commits to exact model weightsPoseidon hash of circuit description
Input bindingProof is bound to specific input featuresHyrax polynomial commitment
Output correctnessOutput is the unique correct result of the computationGKR sumcheck protocol
Non-transferabilityProof cannot be reused for different inputsFiat-Shamir transcript binding

What this means in practice

A bank runs a credit scoring model on a loan application. MOOUSER produces a proof that this specific model produced this specific score on this specific application. A regulator can verify this in 629ms without seeing the model weights or the applicant's data. The proof is mathematically unforgeable — it is impossible to produce a valid proof for a computation that did not happen.

Production Architecture

MOOUSER uses a TEE happy path with ZK dispute fallback. Normal inference runs in a Trusted Execution Environment (fast, cheap). Zero-knowledge proofs are only generated when someone challenges a result.

PathCostLatencyWhen
TEE (happy path)$0.0001Milliseconds99% of inferences
ZK proof (dispute)$0.02SecondsWhen challenged
On-chain (settlement)3-6M gasBlock timeCross-institutional disputes

This makes cryptographic accountability economically viable. You don't pay for proofs nobody checks.

Use Cases

MOOUSER is built for regulated industries where AI decisions have legal and financial consequences.

Credit Scoring

Prove to regulators (CFPB, OCC) that the approved model produced each credit decision. ECOA/FCRA compliance without exposing proprietary model weights.

Fraud Detection

Demonstrate to counterparties that transaction risk scores were computed by the declared model, not overridden manually. Cryptographic chain of custody.

AML Screening

Provide cryptographic evidence that AML models ran on the correct data. Satisfy cross-border regulatory requirements without sharing raw customer data.

Insurance Claims

Verify that automated claims decisions followed the registered model. Reduce dispute resolution from weeks to seconds with unforgeable proof.
