The Competition

OpenAI's Parameter Golf: train the best language model that fits in 16 MB. Score by how well it compresses English text (bits per byte). Lower is better. Everyone gets 10 minutes on 8×H100 GPUs. Same budget, same data. The leaderboard #1 sits at 1.081 BPB.

The One That Worked

ANS Compression (PR #1510)

Worked Compression Information Theory

The question: Everyone uses generic compressors (LZMA, Brotli) to pack model weights into 16 MB. Are they wasting space?

Everyone optimized the model. Nobody optimized the compression. The boring part had the most opportunity.

1. SP8192 Tokenizer on GDN-Hybrid

Failed Tokenizer Architecture

Hypothesis: SP8192 produces 37% fewer tokens. Every transformer uses it. Switching GDN should give -0.03 BPB.

ConfigBPBArtifactFits?

The SP8192 embedding costs 3.7M extra parameters (~1.5 MB). Over budget. Shrinking the model to compensate loses more than the tokenizer gains.

Optimizations that work for transformers don't transfer to architectures with different parameter budgets.

2. GDN head_dim=128 (4 Heads Instead of 8)

Failed Architecture

Hypothesis: 2× state capacity at same parameter count. From first principles: larger key dimension = cleaner retrieval in the associative memory.

8h × 64d

4h × 128d
11.8 GB (44% less)

44% less memory but 0.011 worse BPB. Fewer heads = slower steps = less training.

State capacity is NOT the bottleneck. Training compute (steps per wallclock) is.

3. The Byte-Counting Bug

Discovery Evaluation

Discovery: GDN-Hybrid's eval double-counts leading space bytes for ~65% of tokens.

// BUGGY: +1 baked in, then eval adds +1 again
base_bytes[i] = len(piece[1:].encode("utf-8")) + 1

// CORRECT: no +1, eval adds it once
base_bytes[i] = len(piece[1:].encode("utf-8"))
Reported

1.024
Actual
~1.17 BPB (+14%)
~1.17

When your BPB looks too good, check the denominator.

4. Score-First Compliant TTT

Failed Evaluation Compliance

The problem: Top entries train 6 epochs on val data, then score it. This violates Condition 3 (score-before-update). PRs #1487 and #1488 were closed for this.

My fix: 2-chunk score-first TTT. Score first half. Train on first half. Score second half with adaptation.

Score-first TTT is 0.003 worse than no TTT. Training perturbs the EMA weights. The damage compounds during quantization.

The TTT improvement on the leaderboard may be entirely an artifact of non-compliance.

5. Warmdown Fix

Marginal Training

Three training systems (warmdown, SWA, Late QAT) silently disabled by a timing mismatch. Fixed with ITERATIONS=2200, WARMDOWN_ITERS=400. All three activated. Improvement: -0.003 BPB.

The Pattern

The model works. Dozens of researchers spent weeks optimizing it. There's no easy -0.03 BPB hiding in an architecture change.

The pipeline has bugs. Byte-counting inflated BPB by 14%. TTT compliance affected top entries. Warmdown disabled three systems. The findings were in infrastructure, not the model.

Negative results have value. Each failure narrows the search space for everyone.

What I'd Tell My Past Self

1. Check the eval code before optimizing. I spent days improving BPB that was measured wrong.

2. Run the baseline first. Half my experiments used broken software stacks.

3. Compliance beats cleverness. A compliant 1.11 is worth more than a non-compliant 1.08.

4. $4 experiments are cheap. $150 of undirected experiments is expensive.

5. The boring parts have the most opportunity. Compression, schedules, eval code. Nobody looks. That's why they're wrong.
